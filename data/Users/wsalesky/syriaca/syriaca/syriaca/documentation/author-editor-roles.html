Author and Editor Roles used in The New Handbook of Syriac Literature

The New Handbook of Syriac Literature uses the vocabulary defined here to specify the roles of persons in relation to works. Regrettably, it is not always possible for the editors to investigate the precise roles of authors or editors, especially in the cases where our information is based on datasets that were contributed by external scholars or projects. This means that an author's or editor's role is sometimes not designated when it could be, or that it may be a generic role (such as #redactor or #attributed) when it could be more specific (such as #abbreviator or #incorrectly-attributed). We warmly invite readers to suggest a more precise role by using the "Corrections/Additions?" button on the page for the relevant work and supplying a reliable source for the information.

An author or editor may have multiple roles, so long as the definitions of those roles do not contradict one another. Also, authors should not have roles that are reserved for editors only.

Technical Discussion: This vocabulary defines the values allowed for the role attribute on author and editor tags on Syriaca.org pages.

    role Attribute Definition and Examples
  • The definition for the role attribute in TEI-XML can be found at . See also the examples for editor/@role at .
    • Multiple Values
  • Multiple values should be separated with a whitespace. Multiple values are allowed as long as their definitions are not contradictory. For example, author role="#attributed #disputed" is permissible, whereas author role="#correctly-attributed #incorrectly-attributed" is not.
    • Editor-Only Roles
  • Roles in the category #editor should only be used on editor tags, not on author tags.
    • RDF Properties
  • Relevant RDF properties are noted in this taxonomy. These are intended to give guidance for how the work-person relationship can be expressed in RDF, but they are not exact equivalents and in some cases are much more generic than the Syriaca.org roles. For Syriaca.org work records, the RDF properties for TEI author tags that do not have a role attribute are dct:creator and schema:author. For editor tags without a role attribute, the relevant properties are dct:contributor and schema:editor.
